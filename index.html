About

I am a journalist, a music enthusiast, and an amateur coder.

Building on tools and methods to understand how people use and consume information in their daily lives, I design media products and services.

I currently manage the Design Thinking program at the media startup First Look Media. I joined the company after an amazing experience as a JSK fellow at Stanford University, where I followed a multidisciplinary curriculum involving design thinking and computer science, and did in-depth research and prototyping on new interfaces to access, curate and share information.

Prior to Stanford, I was a member of the team launching the mapping startup CartoDB, and a co-founder of the media non-profit organization porCausa. I also worked for 11 years as a news reporter and editor at Spanish newspaper El Mundo, where I helped grow its digital edition and traveled throughout East Africa and East Asia as a reporter. I also spent three years working in cancer biotech.

Besides my work as a journalist, I'm a guest lecturer at the Hasso Plattner Institute of Design at Stanford, and I've taught full courses on digital journalism, mapping and data visualization at universities in Spain, US and Colombia.
