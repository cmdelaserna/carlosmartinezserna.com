Carlos Martinez de la Serna Journalism. Technology

I am a journalist designing digital products for news. I currently manage the design thinking program at media startup First Look Media, based in Palo Alto (CA). I joined the company after a year as a John S. Knight Fellow at Stanford University, where I was a member of a team prototyping tangible interfaces for data.

I am also a guest lecturer at Stanford’s Institute of Design (d.school) and a co-founder of non-profit organization porCausa, promoting deep dive news coverage on poverty and global development in collaboration with independent journalists and news organizations such as The Guardian and El País.

Before moving to California, I was a member of the team launching mapping startup CartoDB, and a co-founder of single-topic news site Notas de Fukushima, covering the aftermath of Japan’s Tohoku Earthquake. Between 1998 and 2008, I was an editor and health writer at Spanish newspaper El Mundo.

I have lived in Spain, Japan and in both coasts of the US.
