About

I am a journalist, a music & photo enthusiast, and an amateur coder.

I design media products building on tools and methods to understand how people use and consume information in their daily lives.

I currently manage the Design Thinking program at the media startup First Look Media. I joined the company after a wonderful experience as a JSK fellow at Stanford University, where I followed a multidisciplinary curriculum involving design thinking and computer science, and did in-depth research and prototyping on new interfaces to access, curate and share information. I was incredibly fortunate to have this opportunity.

Prior to Stanford, I was a member of the team launching the mapping startup CartoDB, and a co-founder of the media non-profit organization porCausa. I also worked for 11 years as a news reporter and editor at Spanish newspaper El Mundo, where I helped grow its digital edition —which became the largest news site in Spanish language— and traveled throughout East Africa and East Asia as a health reporter. I also spent three years working in cancer biotech.

Besides my work as a journalist, I'm a guest lecturer at the Hasso Plattner Institute of Design at Stanford. I've taught full courses on digital journalism, mapping and data visualization at the IE Business School (Spain), the Knight Center for Journalism in the Americas at the University of Texas in Austin, and Universidad de La Sabana (Colombia).

I grew up in Madrid during the 80s —a time wonderfully portraited in this movie. For the last 20 years, I've lived in Spain, Holland, Japan and in both coasts of the US. I currently live in Palo Alto, California, with my wife and our two kids.
